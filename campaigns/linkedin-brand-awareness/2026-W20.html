Firm-level completion data continues to show implausible patterns. Several firms show high view rates with near-zero completion (Lenhart at 100% view rate / 0.82% completion), suggesting LinkedIn's attribution methodology has not changed. Same likely causes as W19:

  • LinkedIn double-counts completions on autoplay refreshes when the same user is served the video multiple times
  • Attribution drift — completions can be aggregated against firm of record at impression time, stacking the same firm across multiple user sessions
  • "Below reporting minimum" privacy redactions now obscuring multiple firms (~10 in this dataset)
  • One or more of the four active ad sets may have a delivery quirk producing inflated counts

Volume doubled as planned with the budget step-up. The cost shows in audience composition: as reach expanded, the algorithm pulled in a broader, less-targeted audience. Decision-maker title share and ICP company size match both declined materially. Engagement quality eroded slightly (completion rate -1.7pp) while view rate and dwell held steady. This is the volume vs ICP-precision tradeoff playing out in real time.

Key Takeaways
Volume Achieved

4x budget ramp delivered scale

Impressions +119%, views +127%, completions +82% WoW. The budget step-up performed as expected on the volume dimension. Engagement at the surface level (view rate, dwell time) held steady, so the broader audience is still watching.

Targeting Broadened

W19 ICP lock-in did not hold at scale

Decision-maker title share dropped from 54.9% to 42.4% (-10.5pp). ICP company size match dropped from 74.9% to 58.4% (-16.5pp). Completion rate down 1.7pp. The algorithm reached further out to fill the larger budget, sacrificing precision for reach.

Decision Ahead

Refine, hold, or split?

Three options for next week: (1) refine targeting to recover ICP precision at lower volume, (2) hold current targeting and lean on retargeting to filter, (3) split spend — tighter audiences for brand awareness, broader for retargeting funnel. First retargeting performance read expected mid-to-late May before this decision lands.
